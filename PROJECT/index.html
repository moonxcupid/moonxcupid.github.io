Introduction

Starting in June, the Anti-Extradition Law Amendment Bill Movement is already half a year. People participating in the movement by rally, producing and spreading promoting materials, frontline, peaceful gatherings like holding hands and singing songs. According to AntiELAB survey report released by The Centre for Communication and Public Opinion Survey of Hong Kong Chinese University, most of the protestors are between 20 and 24 years old. University students become the main force of the movement. Although classes are suspended due to the unstable factor including the traffic and safety problem, online learning and exam is still processing. We are interested in how they balance the academic achievement and social participation.

About the report

How might we enable a full-time University student to address studying and protesting in order to meet the need of fulfillment of academic achievement and social participation.
