INDEPTH REPORT

SERVEY

PARTICIPANTS

30 female and 20 male completed the survey. Most of them are studying year 3 and year 4.  

School Habits

1 is less involved in school work, and 10 is most involved on school work. All of them attend school over 4 days a week. However, it does not match the degree of participation on school work. Although all of them attend school over 4 days a week, most of them evaluate their degree of participation in school work is 7. It showed that more days attend school, not refer to high degree of participation in school work. Only 3 of them evaluate as 10 (most involved on school work).  

Political Stance 

1 means less supportive of the movement and 10 is most supportive of the movement. According to the chart, over 90% of them evaluated their political stance as 6 or more, it means that most of them are supporting the movement. Only 8% of them evaluate as 1 and 2.

Frequency of joining the protest

48% of them join the protests two days a week. Only 14% of them did not join any protests. Moreover, according to the survey results, Over half of them (52%) are soft anti-extradition bill protesters, and 17 of them are hard anti-extradition bill protesters. Soft anti-extradition mostly attend the peaceful gatherings like holding hands and singing songs.. Hard anti-extradition bill protesters are always stand in the front line.

Balance school work and protest

Most of them (68%) said they can balance school work and protests. From the survey results, we can estimate that, they will do time management to involve on school work or other aspects, instead of only focusing on the protests. However, only based on the survey results, we do not know the details of how they balance school work and protest. Therefore, we choose another way, interview, to talk with the student protesters in order to know more about their time management.
